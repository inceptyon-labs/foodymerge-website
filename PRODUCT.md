# FoodieMerge — Marketing site

## Register

brand — this site IS the product impression; the game itself is on the App
Store. A visitor lands here and leaves either delighted or indifferent.

## Product

FoodieMerge. A cozy merge-2 mobile game for iPhone and Android. Players
inherit Nana Jean's sleepy farm town, Plum Hollow, and rebuild it one merge
at a time. Tap a generator, drop an apple, drag it onto another apple, watch
it become an orange. Fulfill an order from the bakery. The market reopens.
The festival lanterns go up. No combat, no failure states, no scary timers.

Published by Inceptyon Labs LLC, Tampa, FL.

## Users

Three concentric audiences, in priority order:

1. **Parents looking for a game to play with a kid.** The honest center of the
   audience. They're tired of free-to-play games designed to monetize the
   child. They want a sit-on-the-couch-together game with warm art, no
   anxiety mechanics, and content they don't need to vet.
2. **Cozy mobile gamers.** The Travel Town / Merge Mansion / Stardew /
   Animal Crossing audience. They already know the merge-2 loop. They're
   here for the art direction and the no-monetization-pressure pitch.
3. **Existing Inceptyon Labs followers.** Smaller, secondary. They get the
   credibility cue from the studio name in the footer; they're not the
   reason for the page.

## Voice

Warm, plain-spoken, slightly grandmotherly. Like the cottage you visit on a
Sunday afternoon. Confident in the cozy stance: this game is not for
everyone, and it doesn't apologize for being gentle. Never childish, never
twee. The writing should feel like Nana Jean wrote it.

A few sentence textures that fit:

- "Rebuild Plum Hollow, one merge at a time."
- "Two apples become an orange. An orange and an orange become a basket."
- "No timers chasing you. No villains. Just the next merge."
- "Bring it home together."

## Anti-references

What this site is NOT, and what we'd be embarrassed by:

- Free-to-play game marketing tropes: HUGE GLOSSY 3D HERO, percentages of
  off this week, "Limited time offer," gem packs displayed on the landing
  page.
- App store screenshot strips with caption overlays. We're not Roblox.
- Editorial-typographic restraint (display serif italic + tracked uppercase
  labels + thin rules). That's a tech-brand reflex and it'd betray the
  cozy mood.
- Dark mode. The game is sun-warmed cream. Dark mode is wrong here.
- Generic SaaS hero: animated number counters, "trusted by," gradient mesh.
- Anything that reads as AI-generated: stock generic copy, identical
  icon-and-heading card grids, hero-metric template, gradient text.

## Strategic principles

- **The art is the pitch.** Every NPC, every fruit, every building is hand
  illustrated. The site exists to let those illustrations breathe. Type and
  layout serve the imagery, never compete with it.
- **One mood per fold.** Hero is the warm welcome; merge loop is the
  satisfying mechanic; Plum Hollow is the town tour; chains are the
  collector's pleasure. Don't blend them into a single endless scroll
  treatment.
- **No CTAs we can't fulfill.** The App Store buttons are honest "Coming
  soon" tags, not fake links. No newsletter signup, no "join the beta"
  form. If we can't deliver, we don't ask.
- **Build the site the way the game builds the town.** Soft, painted,
  generous. A site that feels like it was made by a small team that loves
  what they're making.
