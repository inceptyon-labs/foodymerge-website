The world will wait while you finish your tea.

Built for parents and kids to play together on the couch. The board is finite. The merges are forever.

  1. No timers chasing you

    Generators have a charge bank. Tap freely while charges remain; when they run out, the basket takes a couple of hours to refill. If you forget the game for a week, it just waits.

  2. Generous, but bounded

    Plenty of coins, plenty of chests, plenty of free rewards. The board is 63 tiles. You'll merge to make space, not to chase a leaderboard. Spending real money is never the point.

  3. Bring it home together

    The art is sweet, the orders are kind, the NPCs say please and thank you. Nothing in here is designed to hurry you, nudge you, or sell to your kid.

Things people ask.

The plain answers, before you wonder.

Is FoodyMerge free to play?
Yes. Free to download on the App Store and Google Play. There are optional in-app purchases — small coin and basket packs you can buy if you want to skip a slow afternoon — but they're never required. The whole game is reachable without spending a cent.
Does FoodyMerge have ads?
No interstitial ads. Nothing pops up to interrupt your tea. The only ads in the game are the kind you choose to watch — a short clip in exchange for an extra basket or a bonus coin. If you'd rather not, you'll never see one.
When does FoodyMerge release?
FoodyMerge arrives on the App Store and Google Play in 2026. We'll add the download links the day it ships.
Is FoodyMerge safe for kids?
Yes. Nothing in the game is designed to upset, scare, hurry, or sell to a child. No villains, no timers, no surprise ads, no gem packs glowing at the top of the screen. The art is sweet, the orders are kind, the NPCs say please and thank you. Kids can play on their own, or sit next to a grown-up and play together.
Does FoodyMerge work offline?
Yes — fully offline. No account, no sign-in, no Wi-Fi required. Your farm town lives on the device. Take it on a flight, take it to the cabin, hand the phone to your kid in the backseat.
What kind of game is FoodyMerge?
A cozy merge-2 game. Two of the same item become one of the next tier — two apples become an orange, two oranges become a cherry, on up a thirteen-step ladder. You use what you grow to fulfill orders from the townsfolk of Plum Hollow, which slowly rebuilds the town: the bakery reopens, the orchard fills back in, the festival lanterns go up. If you've played Travel Town or Merge Mansion, you know the loop. FoodyMerge is the quieter cousin — slow, hand-illustrated, no anxiety mechanics.
Who makes FoodyMerge?
FoodyMerge is made by Inceptyon Labs LLC, a small indie studio in Tampa, Florida. It's a personal project — built by a developer to play with his son, then opened up so other families could play it together too. Every NPC, building, and fruit is hand-illustrated.
